Algebra

Linear Equations

Linear equations are mathematical expressions where the highest power of the variable is 1. They form straight lines when graphed and are fundamental to understanding more complex mathematical concepts.

Key Concepts:
  • Slope-intercept form: y = mx + b
  • Point-slope form: y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)
  • Standard form: Ax + By = C
  • Solving systems of linear equations
Foundation Graphing
Quadratic Equations

Quadratic equations contain variables raised to the second power and form parabolas when graphed. They appear frequently in physics, engineering, and real-world applications.

Key Concepts:
  • Standard form: ax² + bx + c = 0
  • Factoring methods
  • Quadratic formula: x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / 2a
  • Vertex form: y = a(x - h)² + k
Intermediate Applications

Geometry

Triangles and Trigonometry

Triangles are fundamental geometric shapes with three sides and three angles. Understanding their properties is essential for solving complex geometric problems.

Key Concepts:
  • Triangle inequality theorem
  • Pythagorean theorem: a² + b² = c²
  • Special right triangles (30-60-90, 45-45-90)
  • Area formulas and perimeter
Foundation Theorems
Circles and Conic Sections

Circles are perfect geometric shapes with all points equidistant from the center. They are part of the broader family of conic sections.

Key Concepts:
  • Standard form: (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²
  • Circumference: C = 2πr
  • Area: A = πr²
  • Arc length and sector area
Intermediate Formulas

Calculus

Limits and Continuity

Limits are the foundation of calculus, describing the behavior of functions as they approach specific values. Continuity ensures smooth transitions in mathematical functions.

Key Concepts:
  • Limit definition and notation
  • One-sided and two-sided limits
  • Continuity at a point
  • Intermediate value theorem
Advanced Foundation
Derivatives

Derivatives measure the rate of change of functions and are essential for understanding motion, optimization, and many real-world phenomena.

Key Concepts:
  • Definition of derivative
  • Power rule and chain rule
  • Product and quotient rules
  • Applications in optimization
Advanced Applications

Statistics

Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics summarize and describe the main features of data sets, providing insights into patterns and distributions.

Key Concepts:
  • Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
  • Measures of variability (range, variance, standard deviation)
  • Frequency distributions and histograms
  • Percentiles and quartiles
Foundation Data Analysis
Probability

Probability theory provides the mathematical foundation for understanding uncertainty and making informed decisions based on data.

Key Concepts:
  • Basic probability rules
  • Conditional probability
  • Independent and dependent events
  • Binomial and normal distributions
Intermediate Theory
